# HR Analytics Dashboard

## About the Project

This project is an interactive **HR Analytics Dashboard** built in Microsoft Excel to analyze employee attrition and workforce trends. The dashboard provides HR stakeholders with insights into employee turnover, satisfaction, promotions, salary levels, work accidents, and departmental distribution through interactive visualizations and slicers.

---

## Business Problem

Employee attrition is a major challenge for organizations because it affects productivity, recruitment costs, and overall business performance. The goal of this project is to identify patterns in employee turnover and highlight factors that may influence attrition, enabling HR teams to make more informed decisions.

---

## Project Objectives

- Analyze employee attrition across different departments.
- Examine the relationship between promotions and employee attrition.
- Evaluate attrition across different salary levels.
- Compare attrition between employees with and without work accidents.
- Measure average employee satisfaction.
- Build an interactive dashboard that allows users to filter data using slicers.

## Key Insights

- The organization has a total workforce of **14,999 employees**, out of which **3,571 employees have left**, resulting in an overall **attrition rate of 23.81%**.
- The **Sales** department has the largest workforce with **4,140 employees**, followed by **Technical (2,720)** and **Support (2,229)** departments.
- Employees who **did not receive a promotion** recorded significantly higher attrition (**3,551 employees**) compared to employees who were promoted (**20 employees**).
- Employees in the **Low Salary** category recorded the highest attrition (**2,173 employees**), followed by the **Medium Salary** category (**1,319 employees**), while the **High Salary** category recorded the lowest attrition (**79 employees**).
- Employees **without work accidents** accounted for the majority of employee exits (**3,403 employees**), compared to **168 employees** who had experienced a work accident.
- The overall **average employee satisfaction score** across the organization is **61.28%**.

---

## Recommendations

- Develop clear career progression and promotion opportunities to improve employee retention.
- Review compensation and benefits for employees in the low salary category.
- Investigate the factors contributing to high employee turnover in the Sales department and implement targeted retention strategies.
- Conduct regular employee satisfaction surveys and act on employee feedback to improve workplace engagement.
- Continue using HR analytics dashboards to support data-driven workforce planning and decision-making.
